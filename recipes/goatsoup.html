Nigerian-style Goat Meat Pepper Soup Recipe

Description

This is a simple and delicious recipe for Nigerian-style Goat Meat Pepper Soup. The soup is made with tender goat meat, fresh peppers, and a variety of spices for a hearty and flavorful meal. It's perfect for cold days or nights when you want something spicy and full of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g goat meat (cut into pieces)
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger(grated)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Fresh Scotch bonnet peppers (Ata Rodo)
  • 2 tablespoons pepper soup spice mix(calabash nutmeg, uda, alligator pepper, negro pepper, and cloves)
  • A handful of scent leaves
  • Salt and bouillion cubes to taste

Steps

  1. Wash the goat meat and place it in a pot.
  2. Season the meat with salt and bouillion cubes.
  3. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger.
  4. Stir well and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Pour in enough water to cover the meat.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 35 minutes, or until the goat meat is tender.
  7. Stir in the pepper soup spice mix, chopped Scotch bonnet peppers, and black pepper (if using).
  8. Simmer for another 10–15 minutes so the spices infuse the broth.
  9. Taste and adjust the salt and seasoning.
  10. Finish with scent leaves by adding them during the last 2 or 3 minutes of cooking.
  11. Serve the pepper soup hot on its own or with boiled yam, white rice, agidi (corn pudding), or fresh bread.
